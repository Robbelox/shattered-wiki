Biography

Eldraean Rivalry

Divine Mischief

Gods have always had rivalries. A century before the Great Shattering, a skilled blacksmith by the name of Bob Blackfin forged gear for gods in return for enhancements. The god of oceans did not enjoy this meddling of mortal and godly affairs, and cursed some of Bob's tools to turn mortal into dolphin upon touch. As this occurred to Bob and those closest to him, Loki saw something different: new toys to play with. He severely underestimated his toys. Soon, he found himself outsmarted by the foursome. A cave designed as trap became his own cell. The dolphins got ahold of the Heart of the Ocean he was using for torture, and used it to leach some divinity. He was thrown into the caverns below after creating a foursome of deities who would become his fiercest enemies: the Dolphin Gods.

Rise of Elyshi

As quick as they had appeared, the dolphin gods faded away. They kept some remnants of a following after the Great Shattering, but insignificant enough to ignore. Loki however, quickly gained a following through formation of the Elyshi with wide adoption in Kurgan.

Re-heated Rivalry

The Elyshi were not so ignorant, and created rivalry with the most devout follower of Eldraeanism: Regulus Pelagius, grandson of John Pelagius, a Dolphin God. This tension would escalate until one moment in 16 AS, when Loki steps in to pierce Regulus to death. Ikaros, king of Achaea, his father, is devastated. Struck by grief, he makes an emotional decision and declares war on the Elyshi. The Elyshi War is devastating. At the signing of the Treaty of Reconciliation, they were reduced to cult status.
