# Osterwalde ## About Osterwalde was one of two dominant Bastaerni kingdoms to emerge from the wreckage of the [[Great Shattering]], and the state from which the [[Bastaernian Empire]] ultimately grew. It traced its identity back to the legendary [[King Walde]], whose claimed descent from the earth god [[Aemok]] gave the Osterwaldi both their founding myth and their lion banner—a symbol of victory over divine opposition that remained central to their identity long after the tribe had become a kingdom. Like all Bastaerni peoples, the Osterwaldi had spent most of their history locked in cycles of raiding, feuding, and uneasy trade with the colonial powers to the north, their energies as often turned inward against rival clans as outward against foreign enemies. The Great Shattering devastated the old tribal order, and it fell to [[adelaide|Queen Adelaide]] to lead the Osterwaldi through its aftermath—re-establishing the kingdom in depopulated northern territories at enormous personal cost. When she stepped back from leadership, she passed a functioning state to her successor [[Auduin]], who proved to have broader ambitions. Under Auduin and his advisor [[aethelstan-halfhand|Æthelstan]], Osterwalde began promoting a pan-Bastaernian identity, building shared institutions designed to bridge the ancient rivalry with the [[Suari]]. When [[mara-of-suaring|Queen Mara]] of the Suari agreed to dissolve her kingdom and join with Osterwalde, the result was not a simple merger but a transformation: the Bastaernian Empire, with Auduin as its first Kaiser. Osterwalde did not so much end as expand outward, becoming the foundation upon which a unified Bastaernian state was built. === --- ## History ### Ancient Tribes (... - 0) The Osterwaldi traced their origins to the legendary [[King Walde]], who claimed descent from the earth god [[Aemok]]. According to tradition, a jealous goddess whom Walde had rejected sent a mountain lion to kill his kinsmen. Walde wrestled the beast and slew it, taking its pelt as a trophy. The Osterwaldi people subsequently presented the lion on their banner to commemorate this great victory over the gods and to demonstrate the strength and prestige that ran through their veins. The Osterwaldi were part of the broader Bastaerni peoples, a highly tribalised society that inhabited the vast forest lands of the south. The Bastaerni were divided into hundreds of petty kings, each ruling a tribe or even a subdivision of one. This highly decentralised structure led to frequent conflicts between tribes, and when combined with a strongly warlike culture, resulted in near-constant internal warfare. The Osterwaldi participated fully in this cycle of raiding and feuding, leaving their territory primarily to trade with foreign peoples or to conduct raids against those who strayed too close to the forest borders. Throughout their early history, the Osterwaldi maintained a blood rivalry with the [[Suari]] people, another major Bastaerni tribe. This enmity stretched back to time immemorial, rooted in the mythical conflict between the two tribes' legendary founders. The rivalry shaped Osterwaldi identity and military focus for generations, ensuring that much of their martial tradition was honed in conflicts with their closest cultural relatives rather than distant foreign powers. On several occasions throughout history, strong leaders managed to unite the scattered Bastaerni tribes, usually in response to large-scale foreign invasions. These rare unifiers were remembered as harsh and uncompromising rulers, willing to employ brutality to impose unity. The Osterwaldi participated in some of these temporary confederations, but such periods of consolidation never lasted longer than a single generation. Upon the death of the unifying king, tribal autonomy reasserted itself and internal conflict resumed. As northern foreigners, particularly the [[achaea|Achaeans]] and [[varian-empire|Tiberians]], began establishing colonies along the northern coast, the Osterwaldi developed an ambivalent relationship with them. At times, they raided colonial forts and settlements; at others, they engaged in trade. Through this exchange, the Osterwaldi entered wider continental networks, exporting furs in return for luxury goods such as silks, dyes, and metals. The Achaeans and Tiberians frequently attempted to manipulate Bastaernian politics by financing and arming rival tribes, deliberately exacerbating internal divisions. Despite these efforts, neither power succeeded in conquering the southern forests, and the Osterwaldi maintained their independence throughout the pre-imperial period. --- ### Great Shattering (0 - 10) Main Article: [[Great Shattering]] The Great Shattering reached the Osterwaldi and other Bastaerni peoples later than most continental societies, largely due to their geographic isolation and limited integration into imperial systems. When it did arrive, its effects were devastating. The Osterwaldi possessed little resistance to the [[Nasyrik Plague]] that had long circulated through the Tiberian Empire and surrounding regions. Entire villages and clans were wiped out as disease spread rapidly through coastal settlements and inland routes. Mass flight followed, as Osterwaldi survivors moved southward into the Bastaerni heartlands. This displacement often worsened the crisis, unintentionally spreading infection further into previously unaffected regions. In response, several Osterwaldi chieftains implemented harsh but effective containment measures. These typically involved the execution of the infected and the strict isolation of corpses from the wider population. Through such measures, a small number of leaders survived the [[shatterings|shattering]] and absorbed neighbouring tribes and clans that had lost their leadership entirely. These surviving Osterwaldi remained in the southern regions for many years, until the plagues subsided and populations developed partial immunity. The cost of survival, however, was immense. The northern forests that had been the Osterwaldi homeland had been largely destroyed by the divine disasters associated with the shattering. Climatic changes eventually rendered the southern lands uninhabitable, forcing the surviving Osterwaldi back north into territories now largely devoid of human life. From this upheaval emerged the Osterwaldi kingdom, one of two dominant Bastaerni successor states alongside their ancient rivals, the Suari. Both settled along the northern coasts and increasingly turned their attention outward, carrying their ancestral rivalry into a new geopolitical context. --- ### First Lude (10 ~ 100) The Osterwaldi emerged as one of the most powerful Bastaernian kingdoms in the post-shattering world. Its rulers emphasised martial discipline and territorial defence, preserving ancient tribal customs while aggressively contesting borders with rival clans and the Suari kingdom. The legendary heritage of King Walde and the divine favour claimed through descent from Aemok remained central to Osterwaldi political legitimacy and cultural identity. The kingdom maintained the traditional Bastaerni warrior ethos. Education remained rare, with most Osterwaldi born into working families where they learned trades rather than formal learning. Those with greater wealth received basic education, but it remained limited in comparison with other nations. Instead, the Osterwaldi made up for lack of formal education through physical prowess and martial training, learning fighting and hard work above all else. #### Under Adelaide (? - 10) **Queen:** [[Adelaide]] #### Under Auduin (10) **King:** [[Auduin]] King Auduin of Osterwalde faced significant internal challenges during the period that became known as the [[Swift Panic]]. The kingdom remained fractured by competing clans and local power centres, threatening to replicate the endless tribal warfare that had characterised the pre-shattering period. Auduin's advisor, the landowner [[aethelstan-halfhand|Æthelstan]], became instrumental in addressing this crisis. Æthelstan promoted the concept of a broader Bastaernian identity that could encompass both the Osterwaldi and their traditional enemies. He founded a pan-Bastaernian university and proposed jointly administered cities as mechanisms for building shared institutions and reducing conflict. These initiatives represented a dramatic departure from traditional Osterwaldi isolationism and tribal particularism. ##### Unification with Suaring Following the [[Varian–Prythonian War]], King Auduin turned his full attention toward relations with the Suari kingdom. Rather than continuing the ancient blood feud through military conquest, Auduin and his advisor Æthelstan pursued an unprecedented diplomatic solution. Negotiations culminated in a historic meeting between King Auduin and [[mara-of-suaring|Queen Mara]] of the Suari. The two rulers agreed to dissolve their respective crowns and establish an entirely new political entity: the [[Bastaernian Empire]]. This decision represented the first time in recorded history that the Osterwaldi and Suari had peacefully joined rather than fought. During the unification ceremony, the earth god Aemok was said to have appeared, enraged by the peaceful joining of rival peoples and demanding blood sacrifice to sanctify the new order. Æthelstan stepped forward to face the god, expressing both regret and satisfaction at the fulfilment of his vision for Bastaernian unity. When Aemok reached for his divine axe, it was found missing—Auduin had taken it. The king struck Æthelstan with the weapon, severing his advisor's right hand rather than killing him. Auduin declared that this sacrifice would be the only blood spilled for Bastaernia. Auduin was proclaimed Kaiser of the Bastaernian Empire, and Æthelstan became his closest advisor, remembered thereafter as the Kaiser's 'right hand' despite the loss of his actual hand. The Osterwaldi thus transformed from an independent kingdom into the dominant component of a larger imperial structure. #### The Bastaernian Empire Main Article: [[Bastaernian Empire]] Under Auduin's rule as Kaiser, elements of foreign administrative practice were combined with traditional Osterwaldi and Bastaerni tribal values. The empire was divided into semi-autonomous states, each governed by its own leader. This structure allowed the preservation of local Osterwaldi traditions and leadership patterns while creating overarching imperial institutions. The arrangement secured long-term stability and marked the transition of the Osterwaldi from a tribal kingdom to a constituent part of an imperial polity. The Osterwaldi maintained their distinctive cultural identity within the empire, including their reverence for the lion banner and the legendary deeds of King Walde. Their martial traditions and warrior ethos continued to shape imperial military organisation, while their historical rivalry with the Suari gradually transformed into a more cooperative relationship under the unifying framework established by Auduin and Æthelstan. **Notable Osterwaldi:** === --- ## Geography ### Settlements [[Mori]] === --- ## Culture ### Religion #### Aemocism Main Article: [[Aemocism]] The Osterwaldi practiced Aemocism, the traditional Bastaerni religion. Most of the surviving mythology centred on the plights of the gods and their interaction with various other beings, such as humanity and the jötnar, beings who might be friends, lovers, foes, or family members of the gods. The cosmos in Bastaerni mythology consisted of Seven Worlds that flanked a central tree, Læraðr. Units of time and elements of the cosmology were personified as deities or beings. Central to Osterwaldi worship was [[Aemok]], the battle-axe-wielding, humanity-protecting earth god who relentlessly fought his foes. The Osterwaldi's legendary founder [[king-walde|Walde]] claimed direct descent from this deity, making Aemok particularly important to Osterwaldi religious and political identity. Other significant deities included Aemok's son [[Loki]], who craftily pursued knowledge throughout the worlds and bestowed among humanity the runic alphabet; the beautiful, feathered cloak-clad goddess who rode to battle to choose among the slain; the vengeful hunting goddess who preferred the wolf howls of the winter mountains to the seashore; the god whose weather and farming associations brought peace and pleasure to humanity; and the widowed goddess who kept apples that granted eternal youthfulness. --- ### Customs #### Warrior Ethos Education remained rare among the Osterwaldi people. Most were born into poor, working families and learned trades rather than formal education. This differed for those with greater wealth, but even they received only basic education in comparison with other nations. Instead of formal learning, most Osterwaldi compensated through physical strength and combat training, learning fighting and hard work above all else. This emphasis on martial prowess over scholarship reflected the Osterwaldi's forest-dwelling heritage and the constant tribal warfare that had characterised their pre-shattering existence. Even after unification into the [[Bastaernian Empire]], the Osterwaldi maintained this traditional value system, producing skilled warriors and craftsmen rather than scholars or administrators. --- ### Nomenclature Osterwaldi names reflected the tribal, nature-bound heritage of the people, rooted in Old Germanic and Norse influences that evoked strength, kinship with the land, valor in battle, and ties to the divine and mythical. Names were not mere labels but carried aspirations for endurance, wisdom, or prowess—Tancred (thought counsel), Eadred (wealthy counsel), Sigfrid (victory peace)—mirroring a society where survival and honor were paramount. References to animals, elements, and ancestral deeds underscored their insular, warlike culture, with wolf imagery appearing frequently enough to suggest particular cultural weight: Actulf (noble wolf), Coenwulf (bold wolf), Wulfdag (wolf day), Wulfstan (wolf stone). Individuals often bore a single given name, sometimes augmented by a clan descriptor or epithet, emphasising tribal loyalty. Male names leaned toward themes of protection, leadership, and ferocity — Heinrich (home ruler), Leofric (beloved ruler), Ekkehart (hard edge)—aligning with the demands of raiding and defence. Female names more frequently suggested grace, resilience, or natural harmony: Ælfwynn (elf joy), Brihtwyn (bright joy), Ermintrude (universal strength), Ealhswith (temple strength). Both nonetheless shared connotations of unyielding spirit, and the line between them was one of emphasis rather than kind. --- ===
