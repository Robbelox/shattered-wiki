Melwynn Senobryn

About

Melwynn was known as one of the very few people in Prythonia familiar with the works of ancient philosophers, scientists, and historians. Her knowledge was proven to be useful during the construction of Noviodunum, the co-ruled fortress of Prythonia and Achaea. She was the loyal Prythonian subject of the Crown and also a strong advocaate for the democratic principles, however controversial would've been.

Biography

Origins

Coming from the community of slave-pedagogues who traced their lineage to a once prosperous kingdom crashed by Pyrgos, Melwynn was raised in Tiberia. Her parents were teaching a child of their Tiberian master when the Nasyrik Plague started. It took many lives of young Tiberians, and Melwynn's kin was accused of creating the sickness.

One day, the guards of her family's master came and started a massacre, cutting off heads and hands of the slaves and throwing them all in massive fire pits. Only Melwynn and some of her siblings could hide under the corpses of many slaughtered innocent people. She saw her mother's fingers, which were once used to tickle her, burning in the fire. It was a smell she'd never forget.

Mercy was sent upon Melwynn when her siblings and herself were rescued by accident, by Galatai merchants who set sail right after the little slaves were brought to their ship. Unfortunatly, only Melwynn could make it to adulthood since all her siblings lost their lives during the Great Shattering. She served as a translator for king Cedric when they came back from the mountains.

Memoirs

After the short conflict between the Kurgan-Varian-Osterwaldian Union against the Prythonian-Achaean-Suaring Alliance, lady Melwynn contracted a disease. The illness and mourning of her late son Robstern had left her unconscious in Tamaris for the vast majority of time until another war came to the Prythonian lands.

This time the battlefield was split between the Bastaerni and the Galatai: her spouse's nation and her own. This broke her and bedriddened her once again.

When Melwynn woke up on the next day in the captured city of Tamaris, she could only hear the voice of her beloved spouse whispering to her in Achaean tongue. On this day, she, the former Governor of Noviodunum, Senobryn mother and wife, heard of the tragic demolition of Morlan. Lady Senobryn was met in face with despair itself. Her heart was broken. Her newly found motherland had betrayed her and her family. The Senobryn Hill was razed to the ground and the Holy Grove of the Senobryn Family was burnt to ashes. On this day, the Senobryn family lost their homeland.

Palingenesy

After the betrayal, Melwynn stopped coloring hair blond and let the screeching crying sorrow of her dark gray hair be unleashed upon the blood enemies of her family.
