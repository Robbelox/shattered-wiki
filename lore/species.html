Humans
Humans

In the overworlds of both Ayka and Verdan, humans dominate as the most common species, often the sole encounter for many.

While humans are praised for their resilience, both mentally and physically, their tendencies toward ignorance and destructiveness are widely acknowledged. Prioritizing expansion over peaceful coexistence with their surroundings has led to significant environmental damage.

In Iteria, humans stand as one of the oldest species, alongside Faes and Shifters.

Dwarves
Dwarves

Dwarves are inhabitants of mountainous regions or deep caves, where they indulge in activities such as mining and exploring underground caverns. They possess a keen fascination with minerals found in the depths of the earth, particularly gold, and their culture is richly developed, placing high value on history and the origins of their species.

Having adapted to the perpetual darkness of their underground habitats, Dwarves have also developed immunity to the venom of Cave Spiders over time. Their stature is typically shorter to navigate through narrower cave passages, and they experience discomfort and weakness in sunlight due to its intense brightness. While Dwarves boast physical strength necessary for mining significant quantities of stone, they are not inherently inclined towards warfare.

Shifters
Shifters

Shifters trace their lineage back to Faes and Humans. Once possessing the ability to shift into various creatures, their lineage has evolved over thousands of years. Crossbreeding has diluted their blood, stripping them of their shapeshifting prowess.

Despite their name, Shifters can no longer assume alternate forms. Instead, they exhibit one or two physical animal traits, like horns, fangs, or animal ears. Moreover, they retain advantageous Abilities, including underwater breathing and enhanced night vision.

Characterized by individualism, Shifters lead nomadic lives, cherishing their uniqueness. However, they aren't renowned for combat skills; in fact, they often lag behind humans in strength, teetering on fragility.

Fae
Fae

Faes, alongside Humans and Shifters, stand as one of the oldest species, giving rise to Elves and Lizardkin. They're viewed as more magical beings compared to Elves, who lean towards nature-centric pursuits.

There exist three distinct types of Faes: Northern, Southern, and Central. Central Faes, the offspring of the former two, lack their own unique culture and often face societal exclusion.

Faes typically feature pointed ears and may sport hair dyed in vibrant hues like bright blue or magenta. They boast an impressive lifespan of up to 10,000 years, with each century equating to one human year.

Wings, a defining feature of Faes, are often sought after, leading to their frequent hunting and the brutal removal of their wings. The Northern Faes possess grand, crystal-like wings of deep blue, highly prized for their beauty.

In contrast, the harsh conditions of the Southern regions have shaped the wings of Southern Faes to resemble those of dragons: sturdy, brownish, and remarkably resilient. Central Faes possess the most coveted wings, melding the characteristics of both Northern and Southern Faes, resulting in wings of unparalleled beauty and durability.

Elves
Elves

Elves, born of Northern Fae and Humans, dwell closest to nature, embodying the essence of forest high society. Adapted to wilderness survival, they excel in hunting and woodworking. Praised for their skills and beauty, they hold themselves in high regard, often perceived as self-centered.

Tall and adorned with pointed ears, Elves boast a longer lifespan than humans, averaging around 250 years, with every 2.5 years equivalent to one human year.

Half-Elves
Half-Elves

Half-Elves, born of the union between Elves and Humans, find themselves caught between two worlds. Often looked down upon by pure-blooded Elves and burdened with high expectations from Humans, they struggle to find acceptance from either side.

Retaining their pointy ears, Half-Elves lack the strength and elegance of their pure-blooded counterparts. They possess an average lifespan of around 150 years, with every 1.5 years equating to one human year.

Lizardkin
Lizardkin

Lizardkin, descendants of the Southern Fae, are known as a warrior race organized into tribes. They often spend their entire lives within these close-knit communities, though some adventurous individuals may choose to explore beyond their homelands. Despite their fierce reputation in battle, lizardkin have also demonstrated remarkable advancements in fields such as medicine, philosophy, and cultural pursuits like literature and art.

Their distinctive features include brown-yellow or brown-green scales covering their entire bodies, complemented by large, colorful eyes and sharp teeth. Some individuals may also sport tails, spikes along their spine, or, in rare cases, dragon-like wings.

Orcs
Orcs

Orcs are often characterized as ferocious and savage, though exceptions exist. Their upbringing typically lacks intellectual pursuits, not due to incapability but rather a lack of education. Dwelling in nomadic lifestyles, far from civilized societies, they tend to resort to thievery or mercenary work if they become isolated.

Famed for their prowess in combat, Orcs undergo extensive training, with tribal conflicts being a common occurrence within their culture.

Physical traits include tough brown-green skin, prominent fangs protruding from their mouths, and eyes ranging from red-yellow to purple-blue. They typically lack hair and possess a more muscular build compared to other species.

Endermen
Endermen

Endermen, ancient inhabitants of the End, exhibit peculiar behaviors, displaying a semblance of intelligence but lacking true awareness of their surroundings. They manifest in three distinct types: Green, Violet, and Blue.

Green-eyed Endermen represent the pinnacle of intelligence among their kind. Their rarity and diplomatic potential make them highly sought after, though unfortunately, few remain due to relentless hunting.

Violet-eyed Endermen dominate the population, characterized by a seemingly mindless demeanor under the control of the Ender Dragon. They possess little awareness of their environment and communicate through an enigmatic language that defies comprehension.

Blue-eyed Endermen, equally scarce, have broken free from the Dragon's influence through magical or other means. While retaining aspects of their former intelligence, they may occasionally lapse into Violet-like behaviors, such as using their language. Memory lapses are common among this group.

Half-Endermen
Half-Endermen

Half-Endermen are the offspring of a union between one of the three types of Endermen and humans. Unlike their Endermen counterparts, they lack teleportation Abilities but share a common aversion to water, which can cause them harm.

Their physical appearance often reflects their Enderman heritage, with bodies typically appearing as void black. Half-Endermen tend to be taller than average and inherit eyes of the same color as their Enderman parent's eyes during conception.

(V)Illagers
(V)Illagers

The conflict between Villagers and Illagers dates back to ancient times, originating from when both species were part of a powerful, god-like race known as The Vi thousands of years ago.

The division among The Vi arose during a period when Humans faced extinction. One faction sought to protect the humans, while the other aimed to eradicate them. Unable to reconcile their differences, war ensued, resulting in the pro-human faction becoming Villagers and the anti-human faction becoming Illagers. The Villagers emerged victorious, relegating the Illagers to a weakened status on the sidelines.

Half-(V)Illagers
Half-(V)Illagers

Half-Illagers and Half-Villagers are the offspring of unions between Vi and humans. Due to the ongoing conflicts between Humans and Illagers, Half-Illagers are particularly rare.

Physically resembling a blend of their Villager and Illager parents, Half-(V)Illagers typically feature smaller noses and heads. Their appearance can be somewhat unusual, reflecting the merging of two distinct body structures.

Pandafolk
Pandafolk

Pandafolk are a tragic byproduct of Vi experimentation. Originally intended to enhance the value of pandas through weaponization, the experiments only succeeded in creating intelligent, bipedal beings. Dispersing throughout Iteria, Pandafolk have eked out existence in jungles for millennia, organizing into small clans. However, their numbers have dwindled steadily over time, relegating them to the realm of folklore in the eyes of most Iterian inhabitants.

Possessing a lifespan of up to 500 years, Pandafolk are characterized by their soft, valuable fur and panda-like ears, among other distinctive features. Renowned for their huggable nature, they remain a rare sight in modern times.

Undead
Undead

The Undead are known for their stubborn nature, retaining their souls within their decaying bodies after death, rendering them a rare sight. Due to their deceased nerves, they seldom experience pain and exhibit resilience in combat. However, their sluggishness renders them weaker overall.

Sensitive to sunlight, Undead must don protective helmets when exposed to the sun's rays. Their physical forms can vary, resembling skeletons, rotting flesh, or a mixture of both.

Demons
Demons

When a living being perishes, their soul typically finds itself in the Soul Sand Valley of the Nether, indistinguishable among the multitude of other resting souls. However, souls of exceptional strength or those with holy lineage may occasionally extricate themselves from the sand. These souls gradually assume physical forms, comprised of nether materials such as soul sand, netherrack, and blackstone, bound together by the magical essence of numerous souls.

These forms, known as Demons, exist exclusively within the Nether. Unable to endure in the overworld without substantial magic, they require summoners who can share their living magic, allowing the Demon to traverse to the Overworld in its unstable, nebulous body crafted from nether materials and magic, or to inhabit another human vessel. The process of merging a Demon with an existing body invariably corrupts it to varying degrees, contingent upon the ritual performed. Post-ritual, Demons may resemble anything from slightly altered humans to wholly unrecognizable creatures reminiscent of ancient descriptions of monsters.

Demons harness the power of numerous souls, each nearly indistinguishable from the next. However, the most potent souls may retain traces of individuality, manifesting as fleeting, vague memories and abrupt changes in behavior or desires. These memories often appear as static images rather than coherent events, causing confusion and inconsistency in the Demon's behavior.

Goblins
Goblins

Goblins are primarily recognized for their highly chaotic behavior, driven by a singular desire to sow disorder. Despite this, they are not inherently impulsive; they can adapt to structured environments if it serves to foster future chaos. Trust is hard-earned with goblins, but once established, it endures for life, and some may even forsake their chaotic tendencies for the sake of trusted companions.

Their dwellings often lie concealed within caves or forests, away from prying eyes, though some may integrate into towns and cities.

Physically resembling humans but notably shorter, standing at around 4'2", goblins possess greenish skin and typically have black hair, occasionally featuring silvery or white strands. Their distinguishing features include more prominent floppy ears, small body mass, and weaker limbs. Their slight build allows for quick attacks, albeit with limited damage potential due to their fragile nature.
