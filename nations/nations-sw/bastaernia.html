History

The Bast√¶rni people are an example of a highly tribalised society, and the benefits and downsides that come with it. They live in the vast forest lands of the south, leaving only to trade with foreign people for foreign goods. Throughout history, they have been highly insular, keeping to themselves and only conducting raids on those that stray too close to the forest.

Within their society, the Bast√¶rni are split into hundreds of petty kings of a tribe, or even just a portion of such. This highly decentralised system means that wars between the various tribes are rampant, and when combined with their highly warlike attitude, meant that there was little time to fight others between wars among themselves.

However, they have united a few times in history, mostly under a strong leader and in the event of a large foreign invader. These kings who have managed to unite the scattered people have been known to history as hard, strong, but brutal leaders who would do anything to make their plans work. Although, this unity never lasted more than a generation. With the death of the king, the tribes would regain their autonomy and restart the infighting. The only two tribes who have done this, the Osterwalde and the Suaring people, were hated enemies and blood rivals. The two people had hated each other for eternity, their rivalry starting with two mythical founders of each tribe.

Over time, the foreigners from the north started to set up colonies on the northern coast. The Bast√¶rni have had a complicated relationship with them, alternating between ransacking their forts and cities, or simply trading for foreign goods. It is through these trade goods that the Bast√¶rni people have been exposed to the rest of the world, allowing them to trade their lucrative fur trade in exchange for valuable goods from across the seas, including silks, dyes and metals.

The Achaean and Tiberians have been known to finance and equip the tribes, putting them against one another, helping to reignite tensions at every opportunity. However, despite their attempts, neither culture has managed to conquer the southern forests. Every excursion into the Bast√¶rni land has resulted in crippling defeats at the hands of talented raiders, or left with a desolate land without food and cut off from their supply route. As such, the Bast√¶rni have retained their independence throughout history.

The Shattering arrived late for the Bast√¶rni, due to their relative isolation from the rest of the continent and the Empire system. However, when it arrived, it hit them hard. The people had little to no resistance to the multitude of plagues that regularly ravished the Tiberian Empire and the surrounding nations, and as such, their immune system was poor. Entire tribes ceased to exist, as the people fled from coastal areas and towards the deep south that bore their heartland, serving only to spread it further.

Thankfully, a few tribal kings managed to implement systems to combat the plague, which mostly referred to killing those who were infected, and keeping their bodies in isolation from the rest of the tribe. In doing so, they managed to absorb the remaining tribes that had become leaderless.

The new kings stayed in the south for many years, before the plague had finally subsided, the survivors either gaining an immunity to what remained. However, this plague came at an even higher price. The forests of the north had all but been destroyed by the divine disasters that accompanied the Shattering. Nonetheless, the climate had changed, and they were forced from their southern homes and back to the north, which was now desolate of human life.

Two kingdoms have risen within the Bast√¶rni continent, the once rival kings of the Suaring and the Osterwalde. They have settled onto the northern coasts, and now look outward for the first time in history, to the rest of the world to take part in their deadly rivalry. Will they civilise into a feudal system like the other kingdoms? Or will they return to their native tribal roots?

Osterwalde

The Osterwalde clan originated with the legendary king Walde, who claimed descent from the earth god himself. Songs are told about how he managed to wrestle a mountain lion sent (by a jealous goddess that he had rejected) to kill his kinsmen, eventually slaying it and taking its pelt as a trophy. As such, the Osterwalde people present the lion on their banner to commemorate this great victory over the gods, and to show the strength and prestige that runs through their veins.

Suaring

The Suaring clan is descended from the legendary king Suara, who claimed descent from the god of the rivers. Suara is known not only as the first king of the Suaring people, but also as the figure who challenged a god to a bet. He believed himself to be the best swimmer, the one who could hold his breath the longest, and as the best fisherman in the world. The god of the local river with the form of a turtle, however, became offended by his huge pride, and challenged him to a bet. If Suara won, he would be given the finest woman in the world. But if Suara lost, he would drown in the river. The two of them fought hard, taking weeks for each challenge. But, in the end, Suara won, and won against the river god, and went on to create the famous Suaring clan.
