Species: Human
Gender: Male

Kingdom:
Osterwalde → Bastærnia

Played By: Spoon

Æthelstan was born to a small farming family in the Bastærni lands. Throughout the ages, his family had attended to this small farming estate, tending to crops, trading those crops to whoever controlled the land at the time (which changed frequently), and becoming victims to several raids from rival tribes (which happened even more frequently). Despite this, he seemed to have a tongue of silver and could talk his way out of most situations. Many times he was a mediator between the tribes, settling uneasy peace.

For a while, Æthelstan had aspirations like most low-born boys his age: to become a knight, fight great battles, and earn glory and gold. His uncle Alfrid had done the same when he was but a baby, and when he visited his brother Æthelstead, he told the best stories. Later, Æthelstan grew out of his aspirations of becoming a knight when his father's health began to wane; he'd take his father's position for him.

The Shattering hit hard. His father, whose health was already failing, became a victim to one of the great plagues. His mother was bedridden for weeks on end, and no matter how hard Æthelstan tried to care for her, she only got worse. Then young Æthelstan contracted it too, and he thought that he too would perish in the midst of this plague. He pushed through and came out on the other side with interesting news. In all the chaos, some local Bastærni tribes united to form the kingdom of Osterwalde. Æthelstan pledged his allegiance to the reigning monarch and began the task of rebuilding his once great estate, realizing that most other farms had been destroyed.

Auduin
🎀
Kaiser Auduin Wynthoff Der Erste von Osterwalde aus Mori
10 BS -

Species: Human
Gender: Male
Occupation:
Kaiser of Osterwalde
Apostle of Aemocism

Kingdom:
Osterwalde → Bastærnia

Played By: WillMcNut

Auduin Wynthoff was on the younger side when the Shattering hit, and he forgets much of his time before the Shattering. However, he survived because of his father, Askeladd Wynthoff. Askeladd was leading the Waldelach tribe when the Shattering was just starting, noticing disease, famine, and dangers in other tribes. He decided to lead his tribe off the grid. This kept Auduin and the rest of the tribe isolated from the plagues, and they tilled land and set up fruitful farms to prevent famine. The Waldelach tribe was doing very well living in isolation, and Auduin's mother was even pregnant with a boy. His mother, Ylva, was very healthy and had a safe and fruitful birth, welcoming Auduin's younger brother, Thorkfihn, into the family. The birth of Thorkfihn gave Auduin a new purpose; he wanted Thorkfihn to have a regular life. After a few years, Askeladd taught both of his sons how to hunt, build, lead, and trust others. Meanwhile, Ylva taught the two boys generosity, love, and respect, maturing Auduin and Thorkfihn into fine young men.

One day, on a cold fall morning, Ylva saw a wandering man who looked very lost. She went straight for Askeladd and the boys, asking them to see the man and help him if he needed anything. Thorkfihn ran up straight to the man with Godspeed and asked the man for his name and if he needed anything. Thorkfihn was not greeted with a name or request but a forceful blow to his head with a baton. He was unconscious. The man picked him up and ran away, never to be seen again by the Waldelach tribe. This tragedy was the first of many to come upon the Waldelach. Almost right after Thorkfihn’s kidnapping, disease killed half of the Waldelach tribe. Depressingly, Ylva fell ill and was getting worse every day. One day, she had called Auduin over and left him with these words: “Spread peace, protect Askeladd and the Waldelach. And please son, find Thorkfihn.” Auduin swore by these words. He then watched Ylva die in Askeladd’s arms.

Askeladd was suffering from severe PTSD before Ylva fell ill, and this was only increased by her death. Askeladd did his best to guide the Waldelach to success, but he was starting to fail and become resented by the people due to his lust for finding his son. Realizing this, Askeladd decided to give leadership of the Waldelach to Auduin. Auduin accepted by swearing he’d refuse to let another Waldelach die and promised everyone a fruitful life like the old ways they knew before his time. The people accepted Auduin as their leader because of this promise. It was not long after Auduin’s gaining of leadership that his father, too, fell ill. Askeladd knew he was bound for death and prayed for salvation beyond death. Shortly after saying his prayer, he told Auduin that he was going to end his own life to be with Ylva in the forest of the afterlife. Auduin didn’t want to lose his father; he couldn’t let him kill himself. However, it was out of his control, as Askeladd said “lead well, serve your tribe. I love you son,” he took his sword and sliced his body and gut.

Auduin struggled to bury his father and almost fell to depression. But then he heard word from a hunter who had reportedly seen a larger settlement thriving far, far in the distance. Auduin now remembered the words of his father and mother, to love others and care for the tribe. Auduin told the Waldelach tribe to pack up their houses onto their horses, as they were going to be marching for salvation. Thus began Waldelach’s 3-month-long trek to a large settlement of over 10 different tribes. Along the path, Auduin prayed to his gods many times, to see his brother again, for the betterment of his people, and for his parents who had passed. It was after a very long and thoughtful prayer for salvation that he spotted the settlement and hastily arrived.

When the Waldelach arrived in “Osterwalde,” they were met with cold stares and very few open arms. Many people had tainted trusts and closed hearts due to the losses caused by the Shattering. Seeing this, Auduin made another promise, this time stepping onto a small hill and nearly screaming at the top of his lungs: “I will open all of your hearts, peace and power will be restored. Let me, Auduin, be your salvation.” Few people took Auduin seriously at the time, but he worked to fulfill his promise every day by teaching farming, hunting, and building to everyone he met. He also helped anyone who was in need, even if it was beyond Auduin’s abilities to help; he’d do the best he could. And over time, the people started to recognize Auduin as someone to rely on for leadership and assistance. This grew and grew until one day when Auduin left his house, there was a crowd of people asking him to be their king and lead them to peace and power. Auduin was still half asleep, but he accepted with grace and went on to thank every citizen. And thus, uniting the tribes once again, Auduin became King Auduin of Osterwalde. However, Auduin still yearns to find his brother one day, dead or alive; he just wishes to know.

Aurelius
🎀
Aurelius Vara

Species: Human
Gender: Male
Occupation: Emperor

Kingdom: Varian Empire

Played By: Cecilol

Hailing from the prestigious cadet dynasty of the illustrious Tiberians, the Varians are known as one of the most powerful families in the old Empire. Ruling from the city of Vara, they have considerable influence within all branches of government, especially within political and military affairs.

Aurelius was born as a second son to the head of the Varian line, Septimus Vara. Known as a cruel man whose ambitions knew no bounds, and even some rumoured plans to lead a coup against the current Emperor to usurp the throne. As a second son, Aurelius was always treated as a ‘spare’ rather than the ‘heir’. He hardly ever saw his parents, who devoted much of their free time to the eldest son, leaving Aurelius with the servants. Aurelius developed a hatred for his family, and often took to sneaking away from the palace at night, spending the nights in the poor districts of Vara with a gang of children his age, taking part in petty theft and mischief.

When he was discovered sneaking out at night by his father, he was banished from the city and sent to an academy on the mountainous northern border. It was clear the direction his life would take, as devised by his father. A life in the military, bolstering the Varian line by bringing glory to the house, as well as influence within the army, all to improve his brother’s position. The academy treated their students harshly in a militaristic fashion, making the developing boys wake before dawn, study for hours each day, and finish daunting chores. Living in the desolate mountain side was extremely tough, with extremely cold winters, little food, and backbreaking work. Many students couldn’t cope with the new way of life, and by the end of the first season, only half of the year remained.

Despite the hardships he faced, Aurelius continued to survive the work, pushed forwards by his intense hatred of his family and determined to prove them wrong. He excelled under the circumstances, gaining top marks in both classroom academics and physical classes, quickly mastering horse riding and duelling.

When he reached his name day, becoming a man, he was granted leave to travel to Vara, and choose a deity for himself, to watch over and grant him strength. For Aurelius, there was only one choice. He scaled the high staircase leading to the most glorious temple in the city, passing the crippled priests of the goddess, and kneeled down before her altar. He chose the Goddess of War, Beluna as his patron saint, devoting himself to her worship until the end of his days. To seal the pact, he pulled out a knife and marked his face, causing long streams of blood to flow down past his eyes. Despite the intense pain, he did not flinch, the goddess’ power giving him the strength to push past any discomfort. When he rose, he was a different person to before. Now he was a man, with a new aura of strength. With the goddess’ blessing, his destiny had been altered forever.

A few nights later, Aurelius was awoken by the world suddenly shaking. He jumped out of bed, running to the window, just to see the world moving from side to side from some unknown reason. As the shaking grew more violent, screams started to be heard through the darkness, and buildings started to fall, their foundation broken. He ran from the academy along with a few other younger boys, and they hid in the woods, surviving only off of things they could find or catch. However, he knew this couldn’t last. Some day they would have to return. And so, a month later, they ventured out into the world again. When he reached the nearest town, it was empty of life. Only ruins remained of what had once been a prosperous town. It was the same with every town they came across, with only gangs of bandits appearing out of the shadows to loot anything of value from the survivors.

When he finally returned to his home city of Vara, it was no different. Not even one of the greatest cities on the continent could withstand the natural disasters that plagued the empire. It was a shadow of what it once was.

Finally turning his back to the lands of the past, he returned to the mountains, collecting survivors along the way. The land was harsh, and survival hard, but they managed to continue. His leadership saved the lives of many, and he was soon elected as king of what remained of the Varian people. Thankfully, due to its isolation, they escaped the worst of the resulting pandemic that plagued the lands over the next few years.

Now he returns from the highlands, bringing with him the toughest people of the greatest culture known to man. Will he establish an Empire to rival the Tiberians? Or will he fall to some foreign invader like so many have? Only time will tell.

Cedric
🎀
Cedric

Species: Human
Gender: Male
Occupation: King

Kingdom: Prythonia

Played By: Daniël

As a young boy, Cedric fought to survive. His father was a warrior, and so he was taken into the warbands as a child, despite the harsh life. He lost his father young, but fortunately he was well liked, and the men kept him around, feeding him scraps. The lack of food, the cold weather, the long distances to march, they all added up on the growing boy’s development. He would either grow up weak, or stronger than ever.

At the age of 16, he finally underwent the warrior’s rites, becoming a man in the eyes of the Galatai. He was finally able to take part in a raid himself, and to win glory and wealth. They were planning to attack an isolated Kurgan village in the borderlands between the two nations, an easy raid that would slowly bring him into the society. Or so they thought.

They attacked at night, their longbows drawn as they edged closer to the town, hiding behind trees and avoiding the lights. Then came the screams. Men disappeared into the darkness, their lives suddenly extinguished. It had been a trap.

The raid leader had been one of the first to die, an axe flung expertly into his skull, killing him instantly. The raid fell into disarray. There was no longer a leader, and they started panicking, firing arrows blindly into the treeline in the hopes of landing a lucky shot, but instead only bringing on another onslaught of Kurgan. They were surrounded. Cedric had to make a choice; surrender, or fight.

He raised his voice, before shouting at the top of his lungs, ordering the men to form a skein formation, and to advance deeper into the woods, to force their way through the Kurgan lines.

The warband lost half their men, including their leader, but escaped total defeat. In recognition of his efforts, he was unanimously elected as the next leader of the warband.

Word spread of his deeds, as he led the warband to victory after victory, bringing back plunder, slaves, and most importantly, prestige. When the Shattering came, people followed him. He managed to save many people from the onslaught of sicknesses that plagued Prythonia by leading them into the snowy wastelands, his years of experience allowing them to survive in the desolate place. When he emerged, he was not a leader, but a King. The new, and only king of Prythonia.

Conan
🎀
Conan Labelle
15 BS -

Species: Human
Gender: Male
Occupation: Assassin

Kingdom: Kurgan

Played By: WolfShadowRider

Conan was born into the poor side of a very wealthy city. He was taught at a very young age not to oppose the rich. He disagreed with this rule. When at the age of 17, Conan found some likeminded individuals who called themselves "liberators", and they wanted to bring balance to the city's economic climate. "In this way, none will be rich or poor", they said. Conan was skeptical, but also eager to leave his certain life of poverty and servitude. So, he threw away all signs of his past and said, "Show me how.".

These "liberators" would eventually turn out to be a group of terrorists with the intent to overthrow the city's government. These terrorists were primarily made up of orphans and people of severe poverty, though the driving force behind them were the betrayed soldiers from the city's forgotten regimes. Soldiers that outlived their usefulness and were deemed unfit for duty after receiving too many injuries or becoming too old. Conan was amongst the poor, and so he resented the city's hierarchy, thus allying himself with these rebels. But he was only one of many, as he and the other followers beside him learned how to fight without fighting, how to strike from the shadows and avoid all conflict; to kill unseen.

From the ages of 17 to 22, he honed these skills as an assassin and alchemist, learning all sorts of things about how one man could take on an entire patrol of well-armed knights. He was one of the few to survive and be deemed a master. Before he could really turn these skills toward the hierarchy, the Shattering came and destroyed everything. The hierarchy was destroyed, but so too were Conan's friends... his family. He was the sole survivor of the chaos. Whoever managed to escape the city's ruin would be killed in the aftermath. Conan, however, survived.

He bided his time, not knowing how many years had passed while surviving in the wilderness to the north. He had never been in any kind of forestation before but was told what to expect by his terrorist brethren. Using his skills as an assassin and alchemist, he survived by gathering any food, materials and reagents he could find. He learned never to build a home or try to find shelter, since it would merely trap you beneath the feet of a hoard of monsters and insane barbarians. He learned to hunt during the day and hide up in the trees at night. He learned to survive alone, without assistance from his fellow assassins and alchemists. He learned the price and the benefits of solitude and anti-socialism.

Now, here he was, years later and still in the northern forests, surviving. Nothing changed until the day a scouting party of Prythonians set upon him. He easily hid from their sight, having seen them from far away. He was nearly ready to kill them all based on shear instinct when he suddenly awoke from his barbaric post-apocalyptic slumber and realized, “These are real people!” he thought to himself. So, he quickly got a look at their clothing and made the summation that they were some form of nation, due to the markings and symbols on their equipment.

Conan managed to follow them for a few minutes, and as luck would have it, one of their members called for a halt. “Let me have another look at the map for a moment.” their leader said. Conan capitalized on this event and moved in. As they stared at their leader in front of them, dismounting and taking a rest from riding their horses, Conan managed to steal a Prythonian flag from one of the horses’ side-pouches. He then backed away, hiding behind some brush while he worked to make the flag look dingy and torn. He tore it in small, believable ways. He rubbed it against the ground in a rough scraping pattern. The flag began to look almost as dingy as Conan did, after all those years hiding in the northern forests.

Just as the scouting party was to resume their travels, Conan came out in front of them from a nearby bush, holding the flag over his shoulder. In the condition he was in after all these years in the forest, his clothing was unrecognizable, looking more like pieces of dirty wool or parchment. The Prythonian’s first aimed their bows before seeing the flag strung over his shoulder, easily blending with his torn and battered attire. They quickly lowered their weapons and questioned him. As an assassin, Conan found it very easy to utilize practiced universal lies. All he had to do was exchange a few words here and there that gave specific info-textrmation.

Conan spun a tale about how he had lost his way a couple of weeks ago. He claimed to be a Prythonian and was captured by some barbarians but escaped. As a trained assassin, part of his skillset was learning to practice rehearsed universal lies. Lies that apply to multiple situations and can be used anywhere, if a few select words are exchanged to fit the situation at hand. Using these skills, Conan managed to fool the scouting party and they gladly took him back with them.

Conan was finally out of the wilderness and into civilization once more. He was still very unused to speaking to people after all this time, but he had been practicing keeping his social skills for a day just like this. At first, he made an awkward integration into Prythonia, but he managed to fit in after some weeks passed. It was difficult to keep lying, since nobody recognized him, and he had to keep pretending that he was “forgetting” all their customs and daily rituals.

Eventually though, he was found out by the King of Prythonia himself, Cedric. This king apparently knew all his subjects, and Conan was not one of them. The moment he looked upon Conan, he knew something was wrong. Conan, not even getting a moment to say anything, was suddenly set-upon by Cedric’s warriors. Conan quickly became ready to fight, but just before the conflict began, the barbarians Conan had become accustomed to fighting suddenly emerged, attacking from all sides in the city.

Conan managed to prove himself in battle. He fought differently than the Prythonians do. He fought in ways that were cunning and efficient. He evaded most direct conflict and sought out ways to assist his allies. He quickly became a boon in the battle, wielding his Emerald Axe and the occasional potion, he turned the tide of battle and saved many lives that day. The King Cedric look upon Conan a second time, and did not see a Prythonian, but an ally, nonetheless.

Letoius 'Ebbe'
192 BS -

Species: Half-Piglin
Gender: Male

Kingdom: Kurgan

Played By: Letoius

Appearance

In his prime, Ebbe is 5'4 in height, and has long floppy ears that start near his scalp on each side, just above where a normal humans ears would be. Unlike normal piglins, Ebbe's skull isn't as wide, and his eye sockets aren't as round. This is due to only being half piglin, and being what Ebbe had dubbed a "Pig-Man." His skin is a light brown, similar to the colour of dried dirt. Physically, Ebbe is 96 years old, which is considered early middle aged or late young adult for piglins. Ebbe also has two tusks coming from the sides of his mouth at an angle. Each are 6 ¼ centimetres long.

Story

History

Not much is publicly known about Ebbe's history, but it is known he was once a great warrior and leader of the piglins in a large conflict, but fled after the war. After appearing in the Overworld roughly 75 years before The Shattering first begun, he could not survive in the foreign realm. He wandered about the world aimlessly, avoiding towns when possible to avoid conflict, slowly picking up different skills, such as foraging, hunting, reading, writing, speaking, and more.

When the Shattering hit, Ebbe was forced to constantly be on the move. The world, now broken and riddled with monsters just like him, there were now people who would actively seek to hunt them down and kill them. Even if Ebbe tried to speak and plea with one of the humans, it only made them see Ebbe as a bigger monster in their eyes. It was not until early 2020 Ebbe met Haraldr of Uthon and Einar. These two proved to be great friends to Ebbe, as it was them who made Ebbe feel accepted for the first time in over a century. It would be these three who created the Kurgan mining town of Braka Hellir, and Einar and Ebbe would go on to become the owners of the town.

Ebbe's Undead Journey

When Ebbe first arrived in the Overworld, much of his flesh rotted away, as well as his right eye. Normally, when this happens to a piglin, they lose all sense of individuality as their minds rot away with the flesh. However, this did not happen to Ebbe, and his consciousness remained intact after death. For years, Ebbe simply accepted his new way of life. Half dead and running. When Ebbe joined the Kurgan Kingdom, he began to acquire more and more wealth, and as his wealth grew, as did his dreams. Ebbe would spend hours researching and experimenting, trying everything he could to succeed in his goal, and by the thirty-first of August, Ebbe had successfully revived himself to the form he was in when he died. However, the time between was horrible for Ebbe. He lived in constant pain, often drinking to both forget his past and the pain. His mentor was taken by the Shattering. At that moment he felt pure rage. He saw some 20 varians soldiers and killed them all in a matter of seconds.

He was exiled.

His mentor's wolves found him and lead him to the Kurgans who started trusting him. He then got blessed by the gods for his loss, because some say they feared his wrath.

He's a powerful fighter and a nice man when not drunk or angry. He's also a skillful lumberjack, hunter and blacksmith. Wild animals like wolves and bears like him and follow him around.

When he is angry, he is able of killing everyone in his path; allies and enemies alike. He is not suited for ranged combat and is occasionally grumpy in the sense of killing whoever wakes him up that morning. When angry, his body form changes between being wolf and man.
