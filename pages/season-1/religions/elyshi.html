Elyshi

The Elyshi is a structured and expanding religion with a focus on creating detailed spiritual practices and constructing awe-inspiring monuments across the world. Its mission centers on growth, the enrichment of its traditions, and the beautification of the lands through its sacred structures.

Hierarchy and Ranks

The Elyshi religion operates through a hierarchical system comprising five distinct ranks: Disciple, Priest, High Priest, Head Priest, and Apostle. Each rank carries unique roles and responsibilities, although all members contribute to the construction and resource gathering necessary for Temples and High Temples.

Disciples

Disciples are the entry-level followers of the Elyshi faith. Their primary duty is to respond to calls from Head Priests or higher-ranking officials, typically for significant events such as religious wars or grand ceremonies. Beyond this, they are expected to avoid worshipping other deities.

Priests

At the rank of Priest, members begin taking an active role in the faith. Priests are compensated with one coin for every person they successfully bring into the religion. Additional rewards, including coins or diamonds, can be earned by completing tasks listed on the task board or by participating in temple construction.

Head Priests

Head Priests oversee the activities of High Priests and carry greater responsibilities. They are authorized to supervise the construction of Grand Temples (minimum size: 80√ó80√ó80), which are themed around specific gods and require prior approval in council meetings. Successfully overseeing a Grand Temple project qualifies a Head Priest for potential promotion to Apostle.

They are awarded high-tier ceremonial staff and robes, along with a grand ceremony at a completed Grand Temple. Misuse of power by a Head Priest results in branding as a Traitor, with execution mandated.

Apostles

Apostles represent the highest rank within the Elyshi hierarchy. To ascend to this position, a candidate must meet specific criteria:

  • Successfully oversee the construction of a Grand Temple or an equivalent project.
  • Maintain regular activity within the community.
  • Contribute to the religion beyond temple construction.
  • Hold the rank of Head Priest.
  • Recruit at least three new members.

Once these requirements are fulfilled, the candidate is celebrated in a ceremony with mandatory attendance by all Priests and higher ranks. Apostles are granted a personalized staff, their likeness displayed in the Chamber of Apostles, and a unique title. They are also empowered to approve High Priest promotions. Misconduct by an Apostle results in branding as a Traitor and immediate execution.
