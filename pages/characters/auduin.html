Biography

Origins

Auduin Wynthoff was on the younger side when the shattering hit, and he forgot much of his time before the shattering. However, he survived because of his father, Askeladd Wynthoff. Askeladd was leading the Waldelach tribe when the shattering was just starting. He was noticing disease, famine, and dangers in the other tribes and decided to lead his tribe off the grid. This kept Auduin and the rest of the tribe isolated from the plagues, and they tilled land and set up fruitful farms to prevent famine. The Waldelach tribe was doing very well living in isolation, and Auduins mother was even pregnant with a boy. His mother, Ylva, was very healthy and had a safe and fruitful birth, and thus, Auduin welcomed his younger brother Thorkfihn into his family. The birth of Thorkfihn gave Auduin a new purpose, he wanted Thorkfihn to have a regular life. After a few years went by, Askeladd taught both of his sons how to hunt, build, lead, and trust others. Meanwhile, Ylva taught the two boys generosity, love, and respect. This matured Auduin and Thorkfihn into very fine young men at the time.

One day, on a cold fall morning, Ylva saw a wandering man who looked very lost. She went straight for Askeladd and the boys, asking them to see the man and help him if he needed anything. Thorkfihn ran up straight to the man with godspeed and asked the man for his name and if he needed anything. Thorkfihn was not greeted with a name or request, but a forceful blow to his head with a baton. He went unconscious. The man picked him up and ran away, never to be seen again by the Waldelach tribe.

This tragedy was the first of many to come upon the Waldelach. Almost right after Thorkfihn’s kidnapping, disease killed half of the Waldelach tribe. Depressingly, Ylva fell ill and was getting worse everyday. One day, she had called Auduin over and left him with these words: “Spread peace, protect Askeladd and the Waldelach. And please son, find Thorkfihn.” Auduin swore by these words. He then watched Ylva die in Askeladd’s arms.

Askeladd was suffering from severe ptsd before Ylva fell ill, and this was only increased by her death. Askeladd did his best to guide the Waldelach to success, but he was starting to fail and become resented by the people due to his lust for finding his son. Realizing this, Askeladd decided to give leadership of the Waldelach to Auduin. Auduin accepted by swearing he’d refuse to let another Waldelach die. And he promised everyone a fruitful life like the old ways they knew before his time. The people accepted Auduin as their leader because of this promise.

It was not long after Auduin’s gaining of leadership, that his father too fell ill. Askeladd knew he was bound for death and prayed for salvation beyond death. And then shortly after saying his prayer, he told Auduin that he was going to end his own life to be with Ylva in the forest of the afterlife. Auduin didn’t want to lose his father, he couldn’t let him kill himself. However, it was out of his control, as Askeladd said “Lead well, serve your tribe. I love you son,” he took his sword and sliced his body and gut.

Auduin struggled to bury his father and almost fell to depression, but then he heard word from a hunter that had reportedly seen a larger settlement thriving far far in the distance. Auduin now remembered the words of his father and mother, to love others and care for the tribe. Auduin told the Waldelach tribe to pack up their houses onto their horses, as they were going to be marching for salvation.

Thus began Waldelach’s 3 month long trek to a large settlement of over 10 different tribes. Along the path, Auduin prayed to his gods many times, to see his brother again, for the betterment of his people, and for his parents who had passed. It was after a very long and thoughtful prayer for salvation that he spotted the settlement and hastily arrived.

When the Waldelach arrived in “Osterwalde”, they were met with cold stares and very few open arms. Many people had tainted trusts and closed hearts due to the losses caused by the shattering. Seeing this, Auduin made another promise, this time stepping onto a small hill and near screaming at the topic of his lungs: “I will open all of your hearts, peace and power will be restored. Let me, Auduin, be your salvation.”

It’s said Auduin’s eyes were originally hazel and bled purple when he made this promise to the people of Osterwalde.

Few people took Auduin serious at the time, but he worked to fulfill his promise everyday by teaching farming, hunting, and building to everyone he met. He also helped anyone who was in need, even if it was beyond Auduin’s abilities to help, he’d do the best he could. Over time, the people started to recognize Auduin as someone to rely on for leadership and assistance. This grew and grew, until one day when Auduin left his house, there was a crowd of people asking him to be their king and lead them to peace and power. Auduin was still half asleep, but he accepted with grace and went on to thank every citizen.

And thus, uniting the tribes once again, Auduin became King Auduin of Osterwalde. However Auduin still yearned to find his brother one day, dead or alive, he just wished to know.
