    ## History

    ### Origins (10 - 1120)

    #### Mara's Departure (10)
    **Queen:** [[mara-of-suaring|Mara]]

    The people of Mara have their ancient origins as people of [[Suaring]]. When Queen Mara of Suaring and [[auduin|King Auduin]] of [[Osterwalde]] signed the unification of [[bastaernian-empire|Bastaernia]], she transferred her leadership duties to the new Kaiser. Leaving a group of Suari behind in [[Ayka]], she departed with another group in search of a different continent she heard whispers about: [[Quart]].

    - - -

    #### Pilgrimage (10 - 1107)

    The voyage to Quart was completed in a couple of years, only having settled a couple of short times for resupply and recovery. But the [[Quarti Empire]] was not the open land Mara had envisioned. The Quarti Empire, ancient and self-contained, kept its borders sealed to all but the wealthiest merchants. The Mari were turned away.

    They did not abandon the vision. When Mara passed away, they renamed themselves the people of Mara and waited in the manner of a people who had learned to sustain themselves across long stretches of uncertainty. They settled when conditions allowed and moved when they did not. The centennial [[shatterings]] that periodically struck across these centuries carved deeply into Mari numbers and stability, and for three centuries before communities learned to prepare for them, each one tested the pilgrimage's coherence. Hardship produced periodic spikes of violence. In order to keep supplies and provisions high, the Mari pillaged and raided what they stumbled across, though they took only what was needed and avoided excess where possible. This left them with the reputation of a peaceful giant, safe so long as they were content.

    It was during these centuries of semi-nomadic life that the Mari encountered the wandering people of [[Meridion]], themselves displaced and searching for stable ground. A subsection of the Meridi joined the pilgrimage, folding into the Mari over time and becoming a distinct strand within the broader people.

    Between the shatterings, there were long stretches of patience. Generation after generation grew up within sight of a destination they had never seen, sustained by the memory of a queen most of them knew only through story.

    - - -

    #### Culling (900)
    <i>Main Article: [[Culling]]</i>

    When the Culling struck in 900, it came for Quart as it came for everywhere else. The Quarti Empire, which had stood for over a millennium and prided itself on having never fallen to a shattering, collapsed. [[Spectral reapers]] tore through its defences, its final [[Empress Milanka]] died, and the ancient protections that had sealed the continent failed with her. The war of succession that followed shattered what remained, as lords and settlers alike tore into the dying empire's territory.

    For the Mari, watching from beyond borders that had held firm for nearly nine centuries, the Culling was catastrophe and opening in equal measure. The walls had not been opened; they had simply ceased to matter.

    - - -

    ### Entry (1107 - 1120)
    <i>Main Article: [[Quarti Civil War]]</i>

    In 1107, when a hefty civil war was ongoing in Quart, the great Mari pilgrimage pressed forward. Their distance and nomadic ways meant that news of the world at large was slow to reach them, and it came as a surprise to find upon arriving that they were met not again with a mighty united empire, but a war-torn land of blood and steel. They were not deterred. The Mari wiped the local states from the map, took their resources to build a new home, and secured their borders by 1120.

    - - -

    ### Nation of Mara (1120 - 1189)

    #### Under Lord Lonehold (1120 - 1121)
    **Lord:** [[Maverick Lonehold]]

    The early governance of Mara on Quart was fragmented. Power rested with a collection of barons more preoccupied with their own titles and land shares than with the welfare of the Mari people. It was an outsider who changed this.

    Maverick Lonehold was a twenty-eight-year-old traveller who had spent years sailing the eastern islands before finding his way to Quart. He asked to join the Mari and they accepted him. Within two months of his arrival he had disbanded the barons. A speech he delivered in Mara's town square captured the hearts of the citizenry so completely that his elevation to Lord of the Land was swift and near-unanimous. The exact words of the speech were lost to the memories of all who heard it, though the feelings it roused lingered long after. Some attributed his rise to the combined force of his words and the spectacular failure of those who came before him. Others, so moved by what they had heard, believed he would have risen regardless.

    Lonehold governed as a peace-seeker. He established a wage system, opened diplomatic relations with neighbouring nations, and oversaw the construction of highways connecting Mara to [[Lutova]], [[Ondor]] and [[Pakaroo]]. Yet, his tenure was not without difficulty. A prisoner transport and trial for the fugitive [[caradonna-of-the-west|Caradonna]], a wilderness expert who had murdered Lutovan serf [[thrall-chernov|Thrall]] during [[maran-portal-ceremony|Mara's portal ceremony]], ended in her escape from Mara's newly built [[maran-prison|prison]], with four guards killed in the process. A subsequent mobilisation of troops against Pakaroo over the capture of a Mari informant was widely read as rash. His quota system drained the Maran treasury into severe debt. By the end of his first year in power, his reputation had suffered considerably.

    On the tenth day of Mid Winter, 1121, Lonehold was shot during a public speech by a man named [[Matryon]]. He retreated to the stronghold, where Caradonna defeated him in a duel from which he did not recover.

    #### Under Caradonna (1121 - ?)
    **Leader:** [[caradonna-of-the-west|Caradonna]]

    Caradonna of the West had grown up during the pilgrimage itself, orphaned young and raised among children who fell into raiding and violence without adults to guide them. Where most of her peers eventually found their way into ordinary trades, she did not. By the time the Mari reached Quart she was already a figure of notoriety, skilled, paranoid, and prone to violence.

    Following Lonehold's death, Caradonna seized power. She burned every treaty he had signed. The empire she declared was built on confrontation rather than the careful diplomacy her predecessor had attempted, and her reign left Mara in a posture of war with Lutova.

    ## Culture

    ### Symbolism

    The Mari flag bears three gales, each representing a traditional Mari ideal: the top, the breeze of peace; the bottom, the storms of war; the middle, the winds of change.

    ---

    ### Religion

    #### Early Practice

    The Mari carried no unified faith through their centuries of pilgrimage. Their heritage was one of practicality over piety, and for much of Mara's history on Quart, religious life was limited. [[King Gallo]], an earlier ruler, had gone so far as to forbid the public advertisement of religious practice, preferring to keep the people focused on the work of survival and settlement. This began to shift under [[maverick-lonehold|Lonehold]].

    - - -

    #### Pillarism

    A religion of peace founded by a travelling cleric named [[altan-toadstool|Father Altan]]. Pillarism centred on four codependent gods: Benoit the Creator, Mortis the Destroyer, Charie of Justice, and Vide of War, understood as forces in necessary balance rather than rivals. Father Altan departed Quart before his church in Mara was completed, leaving the faith's future among the Mari unresolved.

    ### Customs

    #### Warrior Culture and the Pilgrim's Ethos

    The Mari were not a warlike people by nature, but centuries of pilgrimage had made them capable of war when required. Their military strength was substantial and their policy restrained. They took what was needed and avoided excess where possible, an ethos inherited from [[mara-of-suaring|Queen Mara]]'s founding temperament and tested repeatedly across the [[shatterings]] and raids that sustained them on the road to [[Quart]]. The generation born in the later years of the pilgrimage, who had known nothing but movement and violence, strained against this restraint, and their restlessness shaped the political tensions that defined Mara's early years on Quart.
