History

The misty hills of north-western Tiberia are dominated by the Galatai clans, the original inhabitants of much of the continent. Their hilltop fortresses have borne witness to countless wars fought to resist outside invaders: the Empire's legions, the ever present Kurgan to the west, and their close kinsmen to the west. They are masters of the longbow, the night raid, the sudden wild charge out of the woods. They idolise valour, but especially like it when mixed with a bit of mischief - the cattle thief who can whisk an entire herd into the fog; the champion who dines with a rival tribe, and, regaling his hosts with an anecdote of battle, produces from his bag the skull of one of their kinsmen that he took as a souvenir.

The name Prythonia comes from ancient Achaean explorers, who noted the difference between these people to their western cousins. The Prythonic people would paint their skin different colours, and from this came the name, derived from the Achaean words for â€˜painted peopleâ€™.

The Prythonian people are slightly different to their eastern kinsmen. Being further north, the lands were less fertile, and the people relying on the hilly highlands rather than the lush forests. As such, the people have a much harsher outlook on life, and are much harder in order to survive. Being closer to the Kurgan people of the snow, they also have spent centuries in a series of raids upon their land, resulting in the settling of some Kurgans within their culture that eventually assimilated, bringing with them a collection of new Bastaerni words to the language.

From time immemorial there has been a High King of Prythonia, crowned with great ceremony on the sacred hills of Rhea. Ask any Prythonic chieftain, however, whose "kingdom" he lives in, and he will look around at his hall and in his fields and his pastures, his flock and his retainers, and answer "Mine, of course." Only lately, following the shattering of the continent, have the High Kings made any effort to exert their authority.

Following the Shattering, the Prythonic people were united under a single High King, the first one in centuries. The people benefitted richly from the breakdown of the natural order, and the near total extinction of their hated rivals, the Tiberians. Under his command, and personally leading them, the Prythonians conducted raid after raid into the declining Empire, bringing back gold, food and most importantly slaves to work their own poor soil. For a while, the kingdom grew into one of the richest in the worlds, rivalling ----.

That is, until the impacts of The Shattering spread to the Prythonians themselves. The slaves and food that they brought back to their homeland were infected by the vile plague, and the Prythonic people were decimated by the Nasyrik epidemic. It started with their slaves dying, and then their crops and livestock. Finally it fell upon the ruling classes, now starved of food and forced to flee to escape infection.

The people were taken north by a young noble by the name of Cedric, who fought his way through the apocalyptic world to secure a safe haven for the remainders of his people. They lived among the mountains and forests, trusting no one for years, until finally the world reached a new age.

He led his people down the mountain, and was greeted to a new world. The continents had shifted, water levels had risen, and most importantly, there were no armies to oppose him. Leading them south towards the fertile lands of the seas, he settled his people within the forests of another Galatai people, that are now lost to the ages.

Religion

The Galatai people worship the gods of nature, mainly those of the forests, rivers and highlands that they inhabit.

Those who have a close connection with the gods are chosen to become Druids, who use natural goods to create items to bring the human mind closer to the realm of the gods. These items are usually inhaled or ingested, and induce a magical state that distorts your view of the world, allowing some to even speak with divine beings.

Due to this connection with the gods, the Druids hold a high place in the Galatai society, and are often at the left hand of the king to provide divine judgement.

Battle

Before going into battle, the Prythonic people take the time to bathe, wash, and dye themselves. They have springs throughout the land for washing, and a number of them dot the woodland border where they frequently spar with the southern invaders. To colour themselves, they use a flowering plant that produces a watery-blue dye, which when applied is liable to scar the skin. These scars are a symbol of strength within their society, as an example of the many battles you have fought in and returned victorious

Greetings & Bowstrings

A common Prythonic greeting is to put two fingers to your lips, before raising them up towards the other person, showing your palm to them. This action symbolises friendliness as these fingers are highly important to every man, as their means of stringing their bow, and a symbol of strength within the society. This greeting has been used by the Galatai people for centuries, as a way of showing that you are friendly to one another.

Rings of Wealth

Instead of purses of gold, or hiding your money away, the Prythonic people wear their wealth in the form of rings on their arms. These arm rings are a status symbol for the Prythonians, as a show of how wealthy you are, and as such how many battles you have won. The rings are made up of a vast array of metals, from common bronze to shining gold, and are a show of the feats you have undertaken.
