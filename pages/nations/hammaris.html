Hammaris

History

Origins (... - 900)

The clans of Hammaris roamed the seas and snowscapes of the world for a long time. They were experts at navigating the frozen waters of winter with their longboats. Despite being divided for hundreds of years, the nomadic lifestyle they once led meant they each stuck loosely together in their travels.

The Shatterings over the centuries crippled societies, quaking the earth and bringing monsters forth. Thanks to their hardy nature, the clans survived the travisties that otherwise ruined other nations.

Culling (900)

Much like the other nations, their luck ran short with the horror of the Culling. No longer was it simple storms to endure, but massacres from creatures unknown. The safety of the waters in the harsh times were no longer safe.

The individual clans of Hammaris were scattered, with no more than a handful surviving from each group. They selfishly saw this time as an opportunity to seize power from the others. It was a free for all war on the high seas, and the following months became known as the Month of the Red Sea among the surviving Hammarish.

Once the bloodshed had finally ceased, those who survived saw the folly of their ways. The people agreed to meet in the southern tundras to discuss their further actions, knowing that should they fall into war once more it would be the end of all clans.

After much discussion a pact was made. The pact dictated the clans would not raise blade nor bow against one another. Furthermore, they gave up their life on the waves in place of living in the snowy tundras. Despite being an extremely unpopular choice among the people, it was begrudgingly accepted. The choice was made in order to ensure they stuck together, as the remaining people would have to rely on one another in order to survive the harsh environments of the icy plains.

Battered and bloodied from their turbulent history, the people of Hammaris looked to restore their clans in a new home, under a new, unified flag.
