King Alexander
The Wise of Valdelor
~870 -

Species: Human
Gender: Male
Occupation: King

Kingdom: Valdelor

Born before the time of Arthur of Benton, Alexander grew up in the parted nation that was the once-united Kingdom Valdelor. Growing up, he saw how only a few of the Valdelorians had really adapted to the constant state of change that the kingdom was in, and some tried to, but most had given up. Needless to say, most of them knew a leader to unify them all would be needed to end the chaos.

Then Arthur of Benton appeared. He brought the long-desired peace but was unable to get rid of the chaos at first. To get rid of the chaos, he made a plan: he gave lords and governors land to rule how they seemed fit, with the limitations only being his word and his taxes. Most of the people who were made governors had not been in positions of power before, but that did not stop him. One of the people to become governor was Alexander's father, who was given the lands right next to the capital city to rule.

Arthur of Benton, however, stepped back and retired soon after his goal of unifying Valdelor had been accomplished. A new king was to be chosen; the governors and lords had to decide quickly, since without a king, the kingdom threatened to fall apart once more. Those who had a say in the decision met in the capital city the night Arthur stepped back and chose Alexander to be the new king. The crowning ceremony was held the next morning in quite a rush, as to not give anyone a chance to even notice that there was no king. Alexander's father died of old age only a week later, but the people of Valdelor had trust in the governor's decision, much to Alexander's benefit.

Becoming a Nomad

Lucas was out hunting in Valdelor, just like any other day. As he wandered through the forest, he noticed an unusual shadow on the hill. It was devouring a dead wolf. Judging from its appearance, this... thing wasn't very friendly. He quickly turned the other way, thinking nothing more of it.

Later, he heard a piercing scream that made his ears ache and his skin crawl. It sounded like a girl in distress. Without hesitation, he dropped everything and sprinted over the hill. Unfortunately, he tripped over a concealed rock hidden in the leaves and tumbled down, hitting his head on a tree at the bottom. Darkness enveloped him as he lost consciousness, succumbing to a concussion.

When he awoke, he found himself in a pitch-black cave, cold and restrained against the wall. He called out for help, but received no response. Then, a strange, incomprehensible voice began speaking, sending shivers down his spine. Its abstractness was indescribably unsettling. Lucas remained frozen in fear, unable to move.

After what felt like an eternity, two luminous eyes pierced the darkness. They resembled either cat or owl eyes; he couldn't discern in the dimness. Suddenly, everything became blindingly bright, and he found himself back in his house.

Waking up again, Lucas went to fetch a drink, only to be struck by a headache so intense it brought him to his knees. After a couple of minutes, the pain subsided, but he felt markedly different. Reflecting on the events of the previous day (or what he assumed was yesterday), he realized he could understand the creature's speech, though its words were unrepeatable. A profound sense of urgency washed over him, compelling him to leave as quickly as possible.

In the following days, he info-textrmed his king of his departure for unknown reasons, packed his belongings, and set off into the unknown.
