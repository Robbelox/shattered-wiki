Khalidin

History

Origins (... - 900)

Nomads

The Khalidian were an ancient people who’s ancient nomadic tribes are said to have wandered the deserts of the world since before the Great Shattering.

A reclusive folk, the people of Khalidin were careful to travel away from most settled cultures. Despite being relatively peaceful, ‘civilized’ cultures historically viewed the people of Khalidin as homeless savages. Over the years, the rejection from so many factions led to many Khalidians having an isolated view of the world, believing that their homes were better off without any form of assistance or relation with others.

Oddly, despite such isolationist perspectives, Khalidin became well known for their kindness to foreign visitors. Even when they began building solid foundations, their home was still open to all who travelled the vast sands.

City of Gold

The successors to Khalidin’s nomadic people saw the beauty in settling down and forming homes for their families and religions. They began to be known to build giant monuments to their gods, each monument dripping with gold that seemed to never end. Many such structures have long since fallen to ruin, however ancient temples can still be found half-buried in the sand.

Once the people had settled among the hot sun, their view turned from the sand, to instead what bloomed beneath it; gold. The once thought homeless people of the desert now became the wealthiest nation in the world. With one foot in the past and one in the future, Khalidian rose up with tradition and wealth. Their markets were renowned for unique and hand-made textiles and trinkets, but also beautifully gilded treasures.

Culling (900)

While Khalidin had learned to endure the Shatterings, the Culling brought a new threat. Entire temples and palaces were obliterated, and much of the precious materials and crafts the people valued so much were lost in the mayhem.

What few people remained took it upon themselves to elect a leader, a style of leadership which had never ruled before. Typically the form of leadership in Khalidin was a sultan who passed on their title to their first born. This time an elected leader was needed to unite what few of their people remained. For the first time in centuries, Khalidin had to pick up their roots and move to find a perfect place on which they would be able to regrow safely.
