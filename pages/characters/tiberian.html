Biography

Origins

Tiberian was born in one of the smaller cities of Achaea. Little is recorded of his early life beyond that he rose to prominence as a general, suggesting a military career that predated his political ambitions.

Seizure of Power

The city of his birth had grown wealthy through the slave trade and its position on a principal southern trade route, but that wealth had bred instability. The great houses fought one another for dominance, killing off rivals and hollowing out any central authority. Into this power vacuum stepped Tiberian. He expelled the landed houses, seized their riches, and used that wealth to fund a mercenary army — the foundation of what would become the Tiberian Legion.

Conquest and Empire

With his mercenary force, Tiberian moved against the surrounding Achaean colonies, offering each a simple choice: subjugation or destruction. One by one they submitted. Shortly after consolidating these gains, he crowned himself Emperor of a new nation: the Tiberian Empire.

Legacy

Under Tiberian and his successors, the Empire expanded across much of the continent, its disciplined legions and efficient government drawing towns and peoples into its fold willingly as often as by force. The city of Tiberia became the center of the known world. His direct descendants would hold the imperial throne for generations, until the Great Shattering wiped out the royal line entirely. The Empire he built would itself be destroyed in that same catastrophe, surviving only in the mountains of Vara — reborn, centuries later, as the Varian Empire.
