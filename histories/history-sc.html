Ayka, the Continent of Quart, and the Quarti Empire

Over eleven hundred years ago, the world, Ayka, was struck by an event that came to be known as the Great Shattering. This event brought quakes that tore the land asunder, gave rise to the first generation of monsters, and swept the world with a sickness known as the Nasyrik plague. It was a calamity that many considered to be the end of the world, the doom of civilization. Yet even still, it could not best mankind‚Äôs tenacity.

The Shattering would return once a century, bringing forth new monsters, new quakes, and new illnesses. Time and time again, civilization would fall to its knees, and time and time again, they would pick themselves up. That was, until an event known as the Culling.

Roughly two hundred years ago, on the thousandth year since the Great Shattering, no quakes came. No plagues swept. No monsters rose. Rather, spectral reapers appeared from thin air, indescribable, and dragged mankind through the mud. It seemed as if the world, Ayka herself, wished humanity gone from her fields. All her fields, other than those of Quart.

In a distant corner of the world, the shining pillars of Quart stood tall. Older than the nomadic Khaladians, older than the Kurgan and Hammarish clans that yet sail the seas, older than even the Immortal Empire of Vara that rules in distant lands. The Quarti Empire had been one of the strongest powers in the world since before the Great Shattering, and was oft referred to as the peak of civilization.

Quarti mythology says that the empire was founded by an ancient order of mages, who blessed the land before laying their roots. Whether one believes the tales or not, one fact stands empirical: Quart suffered not a touch of the Shatterings that plagued Ayka. The millennia old protection that the continent lavished in was unable to withstand the onslaught of horrid reapers, and the final Empress of Quart, Milanka, was embraced by the cold arms of death.
