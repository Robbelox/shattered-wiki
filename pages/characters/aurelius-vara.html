Biography

Early Life

Hailing from the prestigious cadet dynasty of the illustrious Tiberians, the Varians were known as one of the most powerful families in the old Empire. Ruling from the city of Vara, they had considerable influence within all branches of government, especially within political and military affairs.

Aurelius was born as a second son to the head of the Varian line, Septimus Vara. Known as a cruel man whose ambitions knew no bounds, Septimus harbored rumored plans to lead a coup against the current Emperor to usurp the throne. As a second son, Aurelius was always treated as a 'spare' rather than the 'heir'. He hardly ever saw his parents, who devoted much of their free time to the eldest son, leaving Aurelius with the servants. Aurelius developed a hatred for his family, and often took to sneaking away from the palace at night, spending his nights in the poor districts of Vara with a gang of children his age, taking part in petty theft and mischief.

When he was discovered sneaking out at night by his father, he was banished from the city and sent to an academy on the mountainous northern border. It was clear the direction his life would take, as devised by his father: a life in the military, bolstering the Varian line by bringing glory to the house, as well as influence within the army, all to improve his brother's position. The academy treated their students harshly in a militaristic fashion, making the developing boys wake before dawn, study for hours each day, and finish daunting chores. Living in the desolate mountainside was extremely difficult, with extremely cold winters, little food, and backbreaking work. Many students could not cope with the new way of life, and by the end of the first season, only half of the year remained.

Despite the hardships he faced, Aurelius continued to survive the work, pushed forward by his intense hatred of his family and determined to prove them wrong. He excelled under the circumstances, gaining top marks in both classroom academics and physical classes, quickly mastering horse riding and dueling.

The Goddess's Blessing

When he reached his name day, becoming a man, he was granted leave to travel to Vara and choose a deity for himself, to watch over and grant him strength. For Aurelius, there was only one choice. He scaled the high staircase leading to the most glorious temple in the city, passing the crippled priests of the goddess, and knelt down before her altar. He chose the Goddess of War, Beluna, as his patron saint, devoting himself to her worship until the end of his days. To seal the pact, he pulled out a knife and marked his face, causing long streams of blood to flow down past his eyes. Despite the intense pain, he did not flinch, the goddess's power giving him the strength to push past any discomfort. When he rose, he was a different person than before. Now he was a man, with a new aura of strength. With the goddess's blessing, his destiny had been altered forever.

The Shattering and Rise to Power

A few nights later, Aurelius was awoken by the world suddenly shaking. He jumped out of bed, running to the window, only to see the world moving from side to side for some unknown reason. As the shaking grew more violent, screams started to be heard through the darkness, and buildings began to fall, their foundations broken. He ran from the academy along with a few other younger boys, and they hid in the woods, surviving only off of things they could find or catch. However, he knew this could not last. Some day they would have to return. And so, a month later, they ventured out into the world again. When he reached the nearest town, it was empty of life. Only ruins remained of what had once been a prosperous settlement. It was the same with every town they came across, with only gangs of bandits appearing out of the shadows to loot anything of value from the survivors.

When he finally returned to his home city of Vara, it was no different. Not even one of the greatest cities on the continent could withstand the natural disasters that plagued the empire. It was a shadow of what it once was.

Finally turning his back to the lands of the past, he returned to the mountains, collecting survivors along the way. The land was harsh, and survival difficult, but they managed to continue. His leadership saved the lives of many, and he was soon elected as the first Emperor of what remained of the Varian people. The city of Vara, having withstood the ravages of the Shattering due to its mountainous location, became the first capital of the Varian Empire. Thankfully, due to its isolation, they escaped the worst of the resulting Nasyrik pandemic that plagued the lands over the following years.

Aurelius led his people from the highlands, bringing with him survivors of the greatest catastrophe the world had known. The Varian Empire, reborn like a phoenix from the ashes of Tiberia, began its long struggle to reclaim what was once theirs and re-establish their prominence in a shattered world.

Stepping Down

On the 2nd of Early Summer, 10AE, Aurelius Vara stepped down as Emperor of the Varian Empire. He appointed Julius sapienti ex Vara as his successor. The reasons for his abdication remained largely private, though some speculated that the burdens of rebuilding an empire from ruins had taken their toll. Regardless, his transition of power was peaceful and orderly, reflecting the Varian commitment to stability and proper succession.
